Why Your Barbershop Needs Online Booking

If you're still running your barbershop on walk-ins and WhatsApp, here's what you're missing:

  • Walk-ins are unpredictable. You never know if Tuesday will be dead or slammed. Online booking gives your customers visibility into your availability and fills gaps in your schedule.
  • Missed calls = missed bookings. When you're with a client, every incoming call goes to voicemail. How many of those call you back? Studies show barbershops lose 20-30% of potential bookings to missed calls.
  • WhatsApp chaos. Managing bookings across DMs, texts, and walk-ins creates double bookings, confusion, and lost revenue.
  • No deposits = no-shows. Without a way to collect deposits automatically, you're at the mercy of clients who book and don't show.

The NZ Barbershop Market

Barbering in New Zealand has undergone a renaissance. Since the 2000s, the number of barbershops has grown significantly as men's grooming has become a cultural phenomenon driven by social media, changing fashion, and a new generation of barbers who blend traditional techniques with modern style.

$1.7B New Zealand hair and beauty services market size (IBISWorld, 2025)

Key market insights:

  • Barbering is one of the fastest-growing segments of the NZ hair and beauty industry. Government reports confirm barbering has "seen significant growth in New Zealand over the last 20 years."
  • Low barriers to entry mean high competition. The number of hairdressing and beauty businesses has grown faster than the total NZ business average since 2015. Online booking is how you differentiate.
  • Independent shops dominate. Large chains hold an estimated 10-15% of the market. The rest are individual barbershops and small studios — your competitive advantage is speed and personalisation, and digital tools amplify that.
  • The modern barbershop customer expects digital. They book everything else online — restaurants, flights, doctors. Your barbershop should be no different.
  • 60% of barbers are formally qualified in NZ, with the proportion lower among barbers than hairdressers. The industry is increasingly seen as a creative, attractive career.

No-Shows Are Killing Your Revenue

Barbershops have one of the highest no-show rates in the service industry. According to Zenoti's 2025 benchmark report, barbershops see a 4% no-show rate and a 2% cancellation rate. While that might sound low, for a busy shop doing 30 cuts a day at $45 each, that's $1,350 in lost revenue per week — over $70,000 a year.

4% Average barbershop no-show rate (Zenoti, 2025). Higher for first-time clients.

The real problem? First-time clients are 3x more likely to no-show than regulars. Without a deposit system, every new customer booking is a gamble. BookrHub solves this by letting you require a deposit for new clients while waiving it for your regulars.
