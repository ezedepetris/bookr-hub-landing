The Real Cost of Phone-Based Booking

If you're still managing bookings by phone, WhatsApp, or walk-ins, you're leaving money on the table. Here's what it's actually costing you:

  • Missed calls = missed revenue. Studies show small businesses miss up to 30% of incoming calls. Every missed call is a potential booking you'll never get back.
  • Hours lost to admin. The average salon owner spends 6-8 hours per week on phone bookings and scheduling. That's a full workday every week lost to admin.
  • Double bookings and errors. Managing bookings across multiple channels (phone, Instagram, Facebook, walk-ins) guarantees mistakes. Each double booking means an angry customer.
  • No way to collect deposits. Without integrated payments, asking for a deposit means awkward bank transfers. Most businesses just don't bother — and then they pay the price in no-shows.

The NZ Market Opportunity

New Zealand's hair and beauty industry is projected to generate over $1 billion in revenue in 2025, with the broader hair and beauty services market valued at $1.7 billion. Despite a -2.0% dip in 2025 due to cost-of-living pressures, the market has grown at a 1.9% CAGR from 2020-2025 and is projected to bounce back at 2-3% annual growth through 2030.

$1.7B New Zealand hair and beauty services market size in 2025 (IBISWorld)

Key market dynamics driving the need for online booking:

  • High competition, low barriers to entry. The number of hairdressing and beauty businesses has grown faster than the total NZ business average since 2015. With more businesses competing for the same customers, those with better digital experiences win.
  • Barbering is booming. Since the 2000s, the number of barbershops in NZ has grown significantly. Barbering is increasingly popular both as a service and a career path, driven by social media and shifting men's grooming trends.
  • Consumers are digitally sophisticated. NZ has 96%+ internet penetration and one of the highest rates of digital commerce globally. Your customers book flights, order groceries, and stream entertainment online — they expect the same convenience from their salon or barbershop.
  • The industry is predominantly small businesses. 85-90% of the market is independent, not chains. Large franchises hold only 10-15% market share. Small businesses that adopt digital tools gain a real competitive advantage.

The data gets worse:

And deposits don't just protect your schedule — they increase revenue. Salons that take deposits also experience an average 9% increase in retail sales and 2% in overall client spending. Clients who prepay are not only more likely to arrive but tend to spend more once they're there.
