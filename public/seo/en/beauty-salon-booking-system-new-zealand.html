Why Your Salon Needs Online Booking

If you're still booking clients by phone, here's what it's costing you:

The NZ Beauty Salon Market

New Zealand's hair and beauty industry is projected to generate over $1 billion in revenue in 2025, with the broader market valued at $1.7 billion. The industry has grown at a 1.9% CAGR between 2020 and 2025, with significant opportunities for digital-forward businesses.

$1.7B New Zealand hair and beauty services market size (IBISWorld, 2025)

Key market dynamics:

  • Highly competitive, low barriers to entry. The number of hairdressing and beauty businesses has grown faster than the NZ business average since 2015. With more salons competing, digital booking is no longer a differentiator — it's table stakes.
  • 87% of the workforce are women. The industry is young, creative, and career-driven. 60% of hairdressers are formally qualified. Professionals are increasingly looking for tools that let them focus on craft, not admin.
  • Small, independent salons dominate. Large chains hold only 10-15% of the market. Independent salons that adopt digital tools can compete with — and outperform — the chains.
  • Cost-of-living pressure is shifting behaviour. The average monthly number of salon visits has decreased. Rebooking rates are declining. Salons with online booking capture clients who are comparison-shopping and booking on convenience.
