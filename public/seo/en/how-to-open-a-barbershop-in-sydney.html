Why Sydney?

Sydney represents one of the strongest markets for barbershops in Australia. The combination of high disposable income, multicultural demographics, and a strong grooming culture creates ideal conditions for both traditional and specialty barbershops.

  • Professional image: CBD workers seek quick, quality cuts during lunch
  • Beard culture: Strong beard maintenance services demand
  • Ethnic hair: Untapped market for specialists in African, Asian, and Middle Eastern hair

Competitive Landscape

Business Registration

You'll need several registrations and structures in place before opening.

1. Business Structure

Choose your business structure:

  • Sole Trader: Simplest, but you personally liability
  • Partnership: If opening with partners
  • Company (Pty Ltd): Most common for barbershops, limits liability

2. ABN Registration

Register an Australian Business Number (ABN) through the Australian Business Register (ABR). This is free and essential for:

  • Registering your business name
  • Claiming tax deductions
  • Invoicing clients

3. Business Name

Register your business name through ASIC or as part of your ABN registration. Consider:

  • Checking availability on ASIC Connect
  • Conducting trademark search
  • Ensuring domain name is available

4. Tax Registrations

  • GST Registration: Required if turnover exceeds $75,000/year (voluntary before)
  • PAYG Withholding: If employing staff
  • Payroll Tax: NSW threshold applies

5. Insurance

Essential insurances for barbershops:

  • Public Liability: $10-20 million recommended
  • Professional Indemnity: Covers negligence claims
  • Property Insurance: For equipment and premises
  • Workers Compensation: Required for employees
  • Business Interruption: Optional but recommended

Licensing & Permits

Operating a barbershop in Sydney requires specific permits.

Required Registrations

  • NSW Business Registration: Through Service NSW
  • Local Council Approval: Change of use application
  • Skin Penetration Approval: Required for any cutting services
  • Food Handler Registration: If serving food/drinks

Local Council Process

Each council has slightly different requirements, but generally:

  • Submit development application (DA) if required
  • Obtain Section 68 approval (sewerage, water)
  • Pass inspection for health and safety
  • Timeline: 4-8 weeks for approvals

Compliance Requirements

  • Hairdresser Registration: While not always mandatory, professional membership is recommended
  • Worker Health & Safety: Safe Work Australia guidelines
  • Public Health: Infection control procedures
  • Building Code: NCC compliance for commercial premises

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to open a barbershop in Sydney?
The total investment for opening a barbershop in Sydney typically ranges from $45,000 to $120,000, depending on your fit-out level, equipment choices, and location. Budget options (smaller space, used equipment) can start around $40,000, while premium shops with full renovations and high-end equipment can exceed $150,000. Ongoing monthly costs typically run $2,000-6,000 including rent, utilities, insurance, and supplies.
Do I need qualifications to open a barbershop in NSW?
While there's no strict legal requirement for formal qualifications to own a barbershop in NSW, if you're cutting hair yourself, most industry bodies recommend completing Certificate III in Barbering. Clients increasingly expect professional qualifications, and proper training ensures you understand health regulations, safety procedures, and quality techniques. If employing barbers, verify their qualifications and registration.
How long does it take to open a barbershop in Sydney?
The typical timeline from concept to opening is 3-6 months. This includes: market research and planning (1-2 months), business registration and permits (4-8 weeks), location scouting and lease negotiation (2-4 weeks), fit-out and renovations (4-8 weeks), and marketing and soft launch (2-4 weeks). Complex locations or development applications can extend this timeline significantly.
What council permits do I need for a barbershop in Sydney?
You'll need several permits depending on your local council: change of use approval from the council, Section 68 approval for water and sewerage, and compliance with skin penetration regulations. Each council (City of Sydney, Inner West, Waverley, etc.) has slightly different requirements. Budget 4-8 weeks for the approval process. It's recommended to engage a builder or certifier familiar with commercial fit-outs to navigate this process.
Is the barbershop market oversaturated in Sydney?
While some inner-city areas have high competition, the barbershop market in Sydney is far from oversaturated. The key is differentiation. Areas like the Northern Beaches, Western Sydney, and certain Inner West suburbs still have opportunities. Specializing in a specific style (fades, beard work, ethnic hair) or target demographic (professionals, students) can help you carve out a niche even in competitive areas.
What insurance do I need for a Sydney barbershop?
Essential insurances for a Sydney barbershop include: Public Liability ($10-20 million coverage recommended), Professional Indemnity (covers negligence claims), Property Insurance (for equipment and premises), Workers Compensation (required if you have employees), and Business Interruption (recommended). Expect to pay $2,000-5,000 annually depending on your coverage level and claims history.
Should I use a booking platform like Fresha or build my own?

Platforms like BookrHub, Timely, or Fresha offer: 24/7 online booking, automated reminders, staff scheduling, payment processing, and customer management. The monthly cost ($50-200) is far less than building and maintaining your own system. BookrHub also offers 0% commission on bookings, unlike competitors that take 5-8% of your revenue.
