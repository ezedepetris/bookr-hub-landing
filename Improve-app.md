ANSWER:
First of all, congratulations on moving from absolutely no visibility to gaining daily impressions, a few clicks, building 270 backlinks, and lifting your Domain Authority! Getting Google to notice a brand-new SaaS product is incredibly hard work, so you have already proven that your foundational efforts are starting to pay off.

However, you have hit two classic "SaaS chasms": The SEO Trap (ranking for terms nobody converts on) and The Onboarding Drop-off (having traffic sign up but leaving before experiencing the "Aha!" moment).

The core diagnosis of your issue is that your SEO strategy and your Product Onboarding strategy are disconnected. Let's break down exactly how to fix both sides of this equation to start keeping those 3–5 monthly signups and scaling your search traffic.

Part 1: Solving the Product & Retention Issue (The Leaking Bucket)
When users sign up but abandon the platform without even adding a service or making a test booking, you are suffering from a Time-to-Value (TTV) issue. The friction between signing up and seeing how the app actually works is too high.

Since you currently have 0 daily active users, your absolute top priority should be fixing this before scaling up traffic. Here is exactly what you need to change:

Implement "Dummy/Demo Data" Instantly
When a user signs up to a blank dashboard, it requires cognitive effort. They have to think of services, input prices, and set up a schedule just to see what the calendar looks like.

The Fix: When they first log in, pre-populate their account with sample data based on a template they choose during signup (e.g., if they click "Barber", pre-fill it with "Men's Haircut - $30", "Beard Trim - $20", an assigned barber, and a few fake appointments on the calendar). Allow them to delete or edit it with one click.

The "Forced" Test Booking Walkthrough
Your app's core value proposition is making booking easy.

The Fix: The moment onboarding ends, launch an interactive widget or popup that says: "Let's do a test run! Click here to see what your clients will see, make a fake booking, and see how it appears on your dashboard." Guide them through making their first test booking. Once they see the email/SMS notification arrive, they will experience the "Aha!" moment.

The "Empty State" Strategy
If their dashboard is empty, use that space to guide them. Instead of an empty chart, show a clear Checklist:

[ ] Add your first service (Takes 1 min)

[ ] Set your working hours (Takes 1 min)

[ ] View your public booking landing page (The magic moment)

[ ] Connect a payment gateway (Optional)

Automated Intercom/Email Sequences Triggered by Inactivity
If a user signs up and doesn't add a service within 2 hours, they should receive an automated email from you.

The Fix: Set up an email saying: "Hey [Name], I noticed you created a BookrHub account but didn't get a chance to set up your booking page. Are you looking to set this up for a salon, gym, or something else? Reply to this email and I’ll personally help you get it live in 5 minutes." (At your stage, doing unscalable, high-touch manual onboarding is your secret weapon).

Part 2: Solving the SEO Issue (Ranking for Intent, Not just Volume)
You mentioned you are averaging positions 16–20 daily but hitting 30+ for most searches. 270 backlinks are a great start, but if those backlinks point to a homepage targeting highly competitive keywords like "free booking system" or "appointment software", you are competing against multi-billion dollar giants like Fresha, Mindbody, and Acuity. You cannot win that broad war yet.

You need a Hyper-Targeted Programmatic and Localized SEO Strategy.

Stop Targeting "Booking System" — Target Specific Roles & Pain Points
Instead of fighting for "Free booking system", create landing pages and blog posts targeting long-tail, high-intent keywords where competition is low but conversion is high:

Keywords: "Best commission-free booking app for solo barbers", "How to set up online booking for a mobile massage therapist", "Fresha alternatives without new client fees".

Why? A barber searching for "commission-free booking app" is much closer to pulling out their credit card than someone searching for a generic "appointment system".

Build Highly Optimized Competitor Comparison Pages
Since your homepage explicitly calls out "Stop Paying 20% to Fresha" and mentions SetMore and Timely, you need dedicated comparison landing pages.

Create these URLs: bookrhub.com/alternatives/fresha and bookrhub.com/alternatives/setmore.

The Strategy: Target keywords like "Fresha alternatives" or "Fresha vs BookrHub". Explain exactly why your flat fee structure beats their 20% commission model. People searching for "alternatives" are highly motivated buyers who are already unhappy with their current software.

Leverage Regional / Local SEO
According to registry data, BookrHub has roots/targets across New Zealand, Australia, and Latin America. National or local intent is much easier to rank for.
Trustpilot Reviews

Target keywords like: "Best booking software for salons in NZ", "Affordable appointment app Australia".

Your Immediate 30-Day Action Plan
Week 1 (Product Fix): Implement pre-populated sample data for new signups. If a user signs up, do not show them a blank screen. Show them a beautifully laid-out salon/barber calendar with fake appointments.

Week 2 (Onboarding Fix): Build an onboarding checklist that rewards them with a celebratory animation when they complete their first "test booking".

Week 3 (High-Touch Outreach): Look at the last 20 people who signed up and abandoned. Send them a personal, plain-text email asking what stopped them or if they'd like a 10-minute Zoom call where you manually set up their profile for them.

Week 4 (SEO Shift): Stop building generic backlinks. Write 3 highly targeted comparison articles (e.g., BookrHub vs Fresha: How to Keep 100% of Your Salon Earnings) and optimize them perfectly for search intent.

By shifting your focus from more traffic to better onboarding, those 3–5 users a month will turn into active users, giving you the case studies and product feedback you need to scale.
